Modernizing developer documentation

Migrating SDK and API documentation out of legacy PDFs and building a new online developer portal with totally reimagined technical documentation.

The problem

A major provider of compliance-grade geolocation solutions and identity verification had a reputation for abysmal documentation.

  • All docs were PDFs. There were dozens of documents, delivered in a variety of ways, maintained by a variety of authors.
  • Docs were often out-of-date, technically incorrect, or simply confusing. The information in some documents conflicted with the information in others.
  • Despite being a solution for developers, there were few code samples and little direct instruction about what needed to be done.
  • Customers made the same implementation mistakes over and over, and virtually every one complained that the documentation was bad.
  • Competitors even cited the docs as one of their own competitive differentiators!

I was hired to build the company's first technical documentation team, take ownership of the docs and the process, and help build a new, modern developer portal that our customers would love. Almost immediately, challenges mounted.

  • After I started, I was informed that all technical writer hiring was canceled and would support a 300+ person product and engineering org alone.
  • The team earmarked to build the developer portal was either laid off or moved to other projects.
  • Repeated organizational changes cycled me through various directors, VPs and departments.
